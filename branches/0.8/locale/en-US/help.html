%ownmail% = Your e Mail Address
%date% = Date and Time (Same like original Mailheader)
%to(mail)% = Recipient e Mail Address
%from(mail)% = Sender e Mail Address
%cc(mail)% = Recipient e Mail Address
%sig% = Siganture (from TB Account Settings)
%sig(2)% = Siganture (from TB Account Settings but without the '--')

Recipientvariables (To)

(When name is not available, will display the email address)

%to% = Recipientname (Default)
%to(firstname)% = Receipient Firstname
%to(name)% = Recipientname

Sendervariables (From)

(When name is not available, will display the email address)

%from% = Sendername (Default)
%from(firstname)% = Sender Firstname
%from(name)% = Sendername

Recipientvariables (CC)

(When name is not available, will display the email Address)

%cc% = Recipientname (Default)
%cc(firstname)% = Recipient Firstname
%cc(name)% = Recipientname

Specialformat
To: %to(name)%{
Cc: %cc(name)%}

The phrase "{Cc: %cc(name)%}" will only be displayed
if variable %cc(name) can be replaced!

Fix variables for dateformat and timezone

(Can be combined with %X:=sent%)

%datelocal% = Date and Time (Locale Format)
%dateshort% = Date and Time short (e.g. 01.01.2000 1:23:45)
%date_tz% = Timezone ("+0100";"+0200"; ...)
%tz_name% = GMT; PST; EST; ...

variables for customized dateformat

(Can be combined with %X:=sent%)

%A% / %a% = Day of week ("Friday" / "Fri.")
%d% / %e% = Day of month ("01 to "31" / "1" to "31")
%m% / %n% = Month ("01" to "12" / "1 to 12")
%B% / %b% = Month ("January" / "Jan.")
%Y% = Year ("1970")
%M% = Minutes ("00" to "59")
%S% = Seconds ("00" to "59")
%H% / %k% = Hour, 24-Hour Format ("00" to "23" / "0" to "23")
%I% / %l% = Hour, 12-Hour Format ("01" to "12" / "1" to "12")
%p% = "AM" or "PM"
%p(1)% = (a.m.)
%p(2)% = (A.M.)
%p(3)% = (AM)

Use the %X:=sent%
The variables above will be normally insert the current values. When you combine this with %X:=sent%, they will set the past values.

Example of a longstring:
%X:=sent%%d%.%m%.%Y% um %H%:%M% Uhr:
Result: 16.02.2011 um 12:15 Uhr:

Additional you can use these variables:
%datelocal% = Date and time (locale format)
%dateshort% = Date and time short (e.g. 01.01.2000 1:23:45)
%date_tz% = Timezone ("+0100";"+0200"; ...)
to set a shortstring.

Example of a shortstring:
%X:=sent%%datelocal%
Result: Mittwoch, 16.02.2011 12:15:25
or
%X:=sent%%dateshort%
Result: 16.02.2011 12:15:25
%date_tz% = Timezone ("+0100";"+0200"; ...)
In both variants you can additionally use the timezone variable.

Notes for HTML templates
Please use only the UTF-8 (Unicode) charset inside HTML templates!

- Special characters: " , & , < , >
Unicode: &quot; , &amp; , &lt; , &gt;

- Umlaut: Ä , ä , Ü , ü , Ö , ö , ß
Unicode: &Auml; , &auml; , &Uuml; , &uuml; , &Ouml; , &ouml; , &szlig;

- New-Lines:
You need to use <br> for new-line, when
'Replace new-line with <br>' is not used.
